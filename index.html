Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives


Dr. Norman Borlaug, third from the left, trains biologists in Mexico on how to increase wheat yields - part of his life-long war on hunger.

Early Life and Education

Neil Armstrong was the eldest of three children born to Stephen Koenig Armstrong and Viola Louise Engel. His interest in flying was sparked at an early age, and he earned his pilot's license at the age of 16. He studied aeronautical engineering at Purdue University and graduated with a Bachelor of Science degree in 1955.

Career

After graduation, Armstrong joined the U.S. Navy and became a fighter pilot. He flew 78 combat missions during the Korean War and was awarded several medals for his service. In 1962, he joined NASA as an astronaut and was chosen to be the commander of the Apollo 11 mission, which landed on the moon in 1969.

Achievements

  • First person to walk on the moon
  • Received numerous awards for his service to NASA and his contributions to space exploration
  • Inducted into the International Space Hall of Fame and the National Aviation Hall of Fame

Legacy

Neil Armstrong will always be remembered as a pioneer in space exploration and for his iconic words when he stepped onto the moon's surface: "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind." His achievements continue to inspire generations of scientists, engineers, and explorers.
